Tourism

What is tourism?

Tourism comprises the activities of persons travelling to and staying in places outside their usual environment for not more than one consecutive year (12 months) for leisure, business or other purposes.

Information on a few animals

Lion

The lion is a large cat of the genus Panthera native to Africa and India. It has a muscular, broad-chested body; short, rounded head; round ears; and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail. It is sexually dimorphic; adult male lions are larger than females and have a prominent mane. Scientific name: Panthera leo Mass: 190 kg (Male, Adult), 130 kg (Female, Adult) Lifespan: 15 – 16 years (Female, Adult, In the wild), 8 – 10 years (Male, Adult, In the wild)

Cheetah

The cheetah is a lightly built, spotted cat characterised by a small rounded head, a short snout, black tear-like facial streaks, a deep chest, long thin legs and a long tail. Its slender, canine-like form is highly adapted for speed, and contrasts sharply with the robust build of the genus Panthera. Speed: 80 – 130 km/h (Running, Estimated) Scientific name: Acinonyx jubatus Mass: 21 – 72 kg (Adult) Conservation status: Vulnerable (Population decreasing)

Tiger

The tiger is the largest living cat species and a member of the genus Panthera. It is most recognisable for its dark vertical stripes on orange fur with a white underside. An apex predator, it primarily preys on ungulates, such as deer and wild boar. Conservation status: Endangered (Population decreasing) Scientific name: Panthera tigris
